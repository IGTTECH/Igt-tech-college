THE REALITY

The 4 Vulnerabilities Plaguing Your Device

Understanding how attackers breach your smartphone is the first, crucial step to stopping them before it is too late.

🦠

Stealth Spyware & Malware

Malicious apps downloaded off the open web that run in the background, logging your keystrokes, viewing your screen, and stealing your passwords.

🎣

SMS & Chat Phishing

Fake links sent via SMS, WhatsApp, or Telegram designed to look exactly like your bank or social logins, harvesting credentials in seconds.

🏃‍♂️

Physical Snatch & Theft

Thieves snatching an unlocked phone directly from your hands in public, granting them full access to your photos, emails, and active sessions.

👁️

Intrusive App Permissions

Seemingly normal apps requesting access to your microphone, location, and storage, quietly collecting user logs and selling them to advertisers.

PRACTICAL STEPS

The 5-Step Device Security Blueprint

Apply these expert guidelines immediately to establish a solid first layer of smartphone defense.

1

Disable Unknown Sources / App Sideloading

Go to Android Settings > Apps > Special App Access > Install Unknown Apps. Block apps (like browsers or file managers) from sideloading arbitrary APKs. Only download apps from verified stores like Google Play.

2

Audit Sensitive App Permissions Annually

Check which apps actively access your Microphone, Camera, and Location. Revoke permission from any app that does not require it to function (e.g. why does a calculator need location data?). Use 'Allow only while using the app'.

3

Block Public Charging Juice-Jacking

Public USB charging ports can transfer malware files while supplying power. Always use a 'USB data blocker' dongle, or configure your phone's developer settings to enforce "Charging Only" mode automatically upon USB connection.

4

Secure Your Clipboard from Data Sniffers

Many apps silently monitor your clipboard history. If you copy a password, credit card number, or secure key, clear your clipboard immediately. Use automatic clipboard purging or security tools to protect data leaks.

5

Deploy an Offline-First Security Suite

While manual habits are critical, malware and phishing operate on sub-second timelines. Deploying an active mobile shield like IGT Security ensures real-time scanning and immediate response to block threats automatically.

COMMON INQUIRIES

Frequently Asked Questions

Clear, direct answers regarding the technical functionality and compliance metrics of our mobile protection suite.

Why does IGT Security require the Accessibility Service?

To provide Link Protection. When you tap a link in a chat or browser, Android delegates the URL to the Accessibility Service. Our app intercepts this URL instantly to verify if it represents a known phishing or lookalike fraud page, blocking access with a secure alert window before you can enter any sensitive data.

We do not read on-screen text, capture key inputs, or harvest any personal data. It is 100% private and offline.

What does 'Offline-First' mean for updates?

It means your scan engines, lock codes, and vault directories live entirely on your device hardware. The threat database is updated automatically whenever you update the app via the Play Store, meaning your phone stays protected even when you have no cellular connection, with zero cloud dependency.

How does the Intruder Selfie work?

If someone holds your phone and attempts to unlock a protected app or your primary secure vault, the app tracks failed passcode attempts. Upon the 3rd failed attempt, the system silently triggers the front camera to capture a high-resolution snapshot. This file is saved directly into your local private vault database with exact timestamps.

Is this application compliant with Google Play Store guidelines?

Yes. IGT Security complies with all Google Play guidelines, including prominent disclosure for sensitive permissions. We seek your explicit affirmative consent before enabling Accessibility, Microphone, or Admin privileges, and we do not sell or leak user metrics. This is a secure, Play-Store compliant utility developed to maintain device integrity.
